Tim Bikmullin

The Internet, a vast expanse of interconnected digital realms, reflects both our global aspirations and local identities. While we perceive it as a singular entity, it is a mirror of our terrestrial existence, adapting and morphing based on our cultural, linguistic, and regional differences.

Through search engines and massive platforms like Facebook and Google, we navigate this digital space, with its architecture continually shaping and being shaped by us. The digital realm is an extension, a reflection, a new layer of our reality.

As the Internet evolves, so does our understanding of space, distance, and connection. We are at once the center and a mere speck in this vast digital universe. As boundaries blur and merge, as the local intertwines with the global, we redefine what it means to be connected, to belong, and to exist in the age of the Internet.
